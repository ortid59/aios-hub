What Gets Built

Six Modules. All Built Around Motion.

Every module maps directly to something you described. Nothing generic — this is built for how Prattify actually operates.

MODULE 01
📄

AI Proposal Builder

You finish a discovery call, notes go in, a fully branded Prattify proposal comes out in 30 minutes. Scoped, priced, and formatted. Jason reviews, adjusts, sends. The 7–10 day gap closes.

What you said: "It takes me 7–10 days to get a proposal out — I'm doing everything myself."

MODULE 02
🧠

Team Context OS

One Claude Team account. Stacy, Marcus, Tyler — all working from the same AI brain with the same client context. No re-explaining. No version drift. One Prattify intelligence layer, shared by everyone.

What you said: "Everyone is using different tools — there's no shared context across the team."
Replaces: scattered individual tools
MODULE 03
🚀

Client Onboarding Tracker

Automated intake form, brand asset collection, kickoff scheduling — triggered the moment a contract is signed. Staff follows the system because the system tracks their steps. Clients stop falling through.

What you said: "The onboarding process exists — the team just doesn't follow it consistently."
Replaces: manual follow-up
MODULE 04
⚡

Motion Intelligence Layer

AIOS reads Motion's Tasks, Projects, Schedules, and Users APIs — then surfaces what actually matters. Deliverables overdue, scope approaching limits, client shoot days, team capacity. All in one view, without leaving Motion.

What you said: "Motion is the core — I don't want to replace it, I want more out of it."
Motion APIs: Tasks, Projects, Schedules, Users
MODULE 05
☀️

Daily Brief

Every morning: proposals that need action, follow-ups that are overdue, onboarding steps that are blocked, team tasks running late. Pulled from Motion + Gmail automatically. Jason starts the day knowing exactly what's on fire and what isn't.

What you said: "I need to know what needs my attention before someone is calling me about it."
New capability
MODULE 06
🔁

Follow-Up Automation

Every proposal that goes out has a sequence behind it. Day 3, day 7, day 14 — personalized check-ins drafted by Claude and queued for review. Proposals stop going cold because nobody had bandwidth to follow up.

What you said: "A lot of times proposals stall — I don't want to chase people but I also can't afford to lose the deal."
